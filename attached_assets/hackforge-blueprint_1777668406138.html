What the market data actually says about hackathons in India right now

02 · Registration
Team Structure & Registration Fees
Fair pricing, clearly explained — with revenue math to back every number

The flat-fee model is easy to explain but bleeds revenue from larger teams. A 6-member team paying ₹500 is ₹83/person — unsustainably low for a ₹20K+ prize pool run by one organizer. The research-backed recommendation is a tiered flat bracket model — different flat amounts per team size, not per-head calculation (which confuses participants).

Team Size Your Original Plan Per-Head (₹100) Recommended Hybrid Per-Person (Hybrid)
Solo (1) ₹300 ₹100 ₹199 ₹199/head
Small (2–3) ₹500 ₹200–₹300 ₹349 ~₹116/head
Medium (4–5) ₹500 ₹400–₹500 ₹499 ~₹100/head
Full (6) ₹500 ₹600 ₹599 ~₹100/head
⚠ Revenue Impact Warning
If 60 teams of 6 register at the flat ₹500 rate = ₹30,000 gross. At the hybrid ₹599 rate = ₹35,940. That ₹5,940 difference funds an entire additional prize or covers Razorpay fees + ops. Don't leave it on the table.

College Bundle (Recommended Addition)

Offer a College Bundle: 3 teams from the same institution for ₹1,200 (saves ₹300+ vs individual). This drives group registrations via college networks, makes the ambassador program naturally profitable, and triggers word-of-mouth within campus communities — the single highest-conversion channel for Indian hackathons.

✓ Refund Policy — Required
You must publish a refund policy before launch. Recommended: 100% refund if event is cancelled by organizer. No refund after submission window opens. Paid hackathons without a clear refund policy see drastically lower conversion rates in India. This is a trust signal, not just legal protection.

Judge Panel Requirements

Minimum 3 judges — never fewer. Final score = average across all judges. This removes single-judge bias. At least 2 of 3 judges must have technical backgrounds (developer, product manager, founder). Non-technical judges score only Presentation and UI/UX criteria. Judges must not know team identities during scoring (blind judging) — prevents college or personal bias. Get written commitment from judges at least 1 week before event and maintain 1 backup judge on standby always.

11 · Risk Management
Risk Control Matrix
Every major risk identified, rated, and mitigated before it becomes a problem
Low Registrations High Severity
Paid hackathons from unknown organizers face massive skepticism in India. Under-registration kills prize pool math and credibility.
Mitigation Launch waitlist 6 weeks early. College ambassador program (10 colleges minimum). Early-bird discount of ₹50 off. Partner with 5+ college tech clubs before launch. Post daily on LinkedIn for 3 weeks pre-launch.
Organizer Credibility Questioned High Severity
India's biggest blocker for paid hackathons is "who is this person and why should I trust them with money?" — especially from students.
Mitigation Post organizer full name + LinkedIn publicly. Attach at least 1 known judge before registration opens. Consider an escrow screenshot or pre-funded prize announcement. Show past work or college projects.
Portal Crash During Finale Medium Severity
Platform crashes have ended multiple Indian hackathons mid-event. Participant trust collapses instantly when tech fails during live round.
Mitigation Load test before event. Google Form as backup submission channel. WhatsApp group as backup communication. Vercel auto-scales; Supabase handles moderate spikes well on free tier.
Judge Backs Out Last Minute Medium Severity
Judges are volunteers. Last-minute dropouts with a 3-person minimum panel breaks the scoring system.
Mitigation Recruit 5 judges, plan for 3. Get written confirmation + calendar invite 1 week before. Always have 1 designated backup judge on standby who has reviewed the rubric.
Plagiarism / Copied Projects Medium Severity
Participants submitting others' work or AI-generated codebases as their own — especially tempting in a paid, prize-linked event.
Mitigation Verify commit history on GitHub. Check video creation date metadata. Ask teams to narrate their codebase during pitch. Disqualify publicly — deters future attempts. Publish anti-plagiarism policy upfront.
Prize Fund Shortage Low Severity
Dynamic prize pool math fails if registrations are significantly lower than projected, leaving the organizer unable to pay promised prizes.
Mitigation Pre-fund prizes from early registrations before committing to a prize tier. Keep prize pool at ≤60% of gross revenue. Publish the scaling formula so participants know prize depends on count.
Participant No-Shows at Finale Medium Severity
Finalist teams not showing up — common in student events especially near exam season. Disrupts entire batch scheduling.
Mitigation Collect emergency contacts. Auto-reminder emails + WhatsApp 2 hrs before. Rule: if team doesn't join by T+15 min, they forfeit. Next team (11th place) moves up automatically.
Prize Distribution Delay High Severity for Trust
Saying "prize within 7 days" and then taking 3 weeks is the fastest way to destroy your reputation. Indian student community talks — word spreads fast.
Mitigation Transfer prize money on the same day as results meet — live on stream. Share the UPI screenshot publicly (with winner's permission). This one action is worth more than any marketing spend.
12 · Honest Audit
Issues to Fix — No Sugar-Coating
Real problems in the current plan that need action before launch
❌ Critical — Trust Gap (Biggest Risk)
Paid hackathons in India face massive skepticism. Participants will Google you. If there's no LinkedIn, no face, no past credibility signal — conversion will be extremely low regardless of how good the event design is. Fix this first, before building anything else. Attach at least 1 publicly known judge or mentor to your landing page before registration opens.
❌ Missing — No Refund Policy
A refund policy is both a legal protection and a trust signal. Required: full refund if organizer cancels, no refund after submission window opens. Without this published upfront, many serious participants will hesitate to pay.
⚠ Revenue Risk — Flat ₹500 Group Fee
6 people paying ₹500 = ₹83/person. After Razorpay fees (~₹12 per transaction), that's ₹71 per participant. Unsustainable for a ₹20K+ prize pool. Either increase group fee to ₹700–₹800 or use the hybrid tiered model. This is a math problem, not a perception problem.
⚠ Marketing Clarity — 8 Hours Expectation
Most successful Indian hackathons run 24–48 hours. Students applying without reading carefully will assume they need to build from scratch in 8 hours. Every marketing touchpoint must say: "You're bringing an existing project and improving it — not building from zero." Misaligned expectations cause dropouts and angry participants.
⚠ No Backup Plan Documented
What happens if your portal goes down during submissions? What happens if Razorpay has an outage during registration? You need a Google Form backup submission link ready, a WhatsApp broadcast list, and a documented incident response playbook before launch day.
13 · Simplification
What to Remove or Simplify
Complexity without value is friction. Cut ruthlessly for Edition 1.
❌ Remove — Auto Per-Member Calculation
Your reference plan mentioned auto-calculating fee as team_size × ₹100. This creates confusion at checkout. Pick one model and commit. The hybrid tiered approach (flat per bracket) is cleaner and simpler to explain in one sentence.
❌ Remove — Over-Engineering the Batch System
For Edition 1 (<100 participants), labels like B1-01, B1-02 are confusing for students. Keep it Batch A / Batch B maximum. Complex labeling kills participation energy before the event even starts.
❌ Remove — Simultaneous Multi-Platform Listing
Don't list on Unstop, Devfolio, AND Devpost simultaneously for Edition 1. Pick Unstop for India student reach. This prevents duplicate registration confusion, double payment attempts, and support overhead across three platforms. Add Devfolio in Edition 2.
⚠ Simplify — The Shortlist-then-Eliminate Flow
For <100 participants: Registration → Shortlist Top 15 → Grand Finale (Top 10). Skip the separate Elimination Round. It adds one extra week and one extra emotional swing for participants. With a small pool, the shortlisting IS the elimination. Add the intermediate round only when you hit 200+ participants.
